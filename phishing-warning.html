Phishing Awareness Simulation

This was a simulated phishing attempt for educational purposes. In a real scenario, your credentials could have been stolen.

How to Identify Phishing Attempts

  • Urgency tactics: The countdown timer was designed to create a false sense of urgency, pressuring you to act quickly without thinking.
  • Suspicious URLs: Always check the website URL before entering credentials. Legitimate sites use HTTPS and have the correct domain name.
  • Generic greetings: Phishing emails often use generic greetings rather than your actual name.
  • Poor spelling and grammar: Many phishing attempts contain spelling or grammatical errors.
  • Requests for sensitive information: Legitimate companies rarely ask for passwords or personal information via email.
  • Fake security badges: The "Protected by SecureSSL™" badge was fake and designed to create a false sense of security.

What You Should Do

If you suspect a phishing attempt:

  • Do not click on any links or download any attachments
  • Do not provide any personal information
  • Report the suspicious email to your IT department
  • Delete the email from your inbox
  • If you've already entered information, change your passwords immediately
