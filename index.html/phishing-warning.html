Warning Signs You Should Have Noticed:

  • Suspicious URL: Real websites have legitimate domains. The URL of this page was not the official domain of the service it was imitating.
  • Fake Security Indicators: The "SecureSSL" badge was not legitimate. Real secure sites show a padlock in the browser address bar.

  • Non-functional Links: The "Forgot Password" link didn't work - many phishing sites have broken or non-functional elements.

How to Protect Yourself from Real Phishing:

  • Always check the URL in your browser's address bar before entering credentials.
  • Look for HTTPS and the padlock icon in your browser.
  • Be suspicious of urgent requests or threats.
  • Don't click on links in emails - instead, manually navigate to the website.
  • Use two-factor authentication when available.
  • When in doubt, contact the company directly using their official contact information.
